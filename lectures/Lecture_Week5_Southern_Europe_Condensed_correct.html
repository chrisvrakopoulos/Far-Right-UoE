South European Patterns

Historical Legacies Matter

How regimes ended:

  • Italy: Mussolini defeated (war), gradual acceptance
  • Spain: Franco died, transition incorporated regime members
  • Greece: Junta collapsed (Cyprus), some integration
  • Portugal: Left-wing revolution, anti-fascist consensus

Effect on far-right:

  • Italy: Longest continuous legitimization (MSI‚ÜíAN‚ÜíFdI)
  • Spain: Absorption into PP, blocked independent far-right for ~40 years
  • Greece: Multiple attempts, crisis breakthrough
  • Portugal: Strongest post-authoritarian stigma, 45-year delay

Key insight: Different transitions create different party-space for the far right!!

BUT: How and why the effect of legacies break?

Key Puzzles from Southern Europe

  1. The Crisis Timing Puzzle - When economic crisis creates far-right opportunities, what determines the timing of breakthrough? Can crisis alone drive far-right success, or is something else required?

  2. The Historical Durability Question - How long can historical legacies (regime type, founding narratives) delay or block far-right emergence? Is there a breaking point?

  3. The Supply-Demand Relationship - Across all cases, demand for far-right politics existed before parties mobilized it. What explains when credible supply emerges to meet demand?

  4. The Mainstream Strategy Question - Do different mainstream responses (isolation, incorporation, accommodation) permanently block far-right, or do they merely delay breakthrough?

  5. The Path Dependency Puzzle - Does the type of regime transition (left-wing revolution vs. right-wing elite transition vs. external collapse) shape far-right trajectories for decades? If so, why?

  6. The Convergence Question - Why do regions with dramatically different historical contexts eventually converge toward similar far-right electoral levels (15-25%)?

  7. The Legitimization Mechanism - What transforms far-right parties from pariah to mainstream? Does it require government participation, gradual normalization, or mainstream accommodation?
