The Normalisation of the Far-Right

Strategic Interaction, Media, and Legitimacy (Week 9)

Dr. Christos Vrakopoulos

The Core Puzzle

  • The Observation: Across Europe, Mainstream Parties (MSPs) have tried everything to stop the Far-Right.
  • The Strategies: They have ignored them, attacked them, and copied them.
  • The Outcome: The Far-Right has moved from the “lunatic fringe” to the centre of power.
  • The Question: Why do “rational” strategies by powerful mainstream parties fail to stop—and often accelerate—this process?

Analytical Framework

  • We will examine three core dimensions of the normalization process.
  • Dimension I: Strategic Competition (The Rational Actor vs. Empirical Reality)
  • Dimension II: Information Environments (The Role of Media in Shaping Preferences)
  • Dimension III: Temporal Dynamics (Stigmatization and Path Dependence)

Dimension I: Strategic Competition

Theory A: The Spatial Logic (Meguid 2005, 2008)

  • Premise: Politics is a market. Parties compete for voters on issues.
  • The Problem: A niche party (Far-Right) owns a new issue (Immigration).
  • The Solution (Accommodation): The Mainstream Centre-Right (MCR) should adopt the niche party’s position.
  • The Mechanism:
    1. MCR steals “ownership” of the issue.
    2. Voters, seeing identical policies, choose the large, competent MCR over the risky niche party.
    3. Result: Niche party collapses.

The Empirical Reality Check (Krause et al. 2023)

  • The Test: Analyzed 350+ mainstream strategies across 12 Western European countries (1976-2017).
  • The Question: Does adopting anti-immigration positions reduce Far-Right votes?
  • The Finding: No.
    • Accommodation does not reduce Radical Right support.
    • In fact, it often increases voter transfers to the Radical Right.
  • The Mechanism: Voters prefer the “original” to the “copy” (Jean-Marie Le Pen).
  • Key Takeaway: You cannot beat the Far-Right by becoming them.

Does it work? The Case of the UK

  • Scenario: UKIP threatens the Conservative Party (2010s).
  • Strategy: Conservatives adopt UKIP’s core demand (EU Referendum/Brexit).
  • Electoral Outcome: UKIP vote collapses (2017/2019).
  • Verdict: Meguid appears right only if we look at the party label.
  • BUT: As Krause et al. suggest, this is the exception, not the rule. And the ideological cost was total.

Theory B: The Legitimacy Trap (Daur 2024)

  • The Counter-Argument: Accommodating a “Pariah” (outcast) party isn’t just a policy shift; it’s a moral signal.
  • The Experiment: Daur (2024) shows that when MSPs adopt Far-Right positions or cooperate, they signal that the Far-Right is “appropriate, proper, and just.”
  • The “Validation” Effect:
    • Voters update their beliefs: “If the Prime Minister says it, it can’t be racist.”
    • Legitimacy extends beyond the MSP’s own voters.

Synthesis: The “Success” Paradox

  • The Double Failure:
    1. Electoral: You often fail to destroy the party. Krause et al. (2023) show accommodation often helps the Far-Right.
    2. Normative: Even if the party dies (e.g., UKIP), you normalise the ideology (Brexit is law).
  • The Trap: By accommodating, you lower the “reputational cost” of Far-Right ideas.
  • Valentim (2021): This is amplified by Representation.
    • Crossing the threshold (5%) into Parliament acts as an institutional “Seal of Approval.”
    • Radical views become publicly acceptable because they are present in the House.

Dimension II: Information Environments

Theory A: The “Mirror” Hypothesis

  • Premise: Media and parties just “reflect” what the people want (Demand-Side).
  • Argument: The Far-Right rises because people are already worried about immigration. The media just reports it.
  • Implication: Censoring the Far-Right or the media wouldn’t change the underlying demand.

Theory B: The Media as “Maker” (Foos & Bischof 2022)

  • The Study: A natural experiment in the UK.
  • The Event: The Liverpool boycott of The Sun (tabloid) after the Hillsborough disaster (1989).
  • The Findings:
    • Merseyside (boycott area) became significantly less Eurosceptic than comparable areas.
    • Why? Because they weren’t exposed to decades of anti-EU crusade by The Sun.
  • The Implication: The media doesn’t just mirror demand; it manufactures it.

Connect to Normalisation

  • If media campaigns can drive Euroscepticism (as Foos & Bischof show), what happens when mainstream media treats the Far-Right as “normal”?
  • Media Mainstreaming:
    • Giving them a platform (BBC “Question Time” effect).
    • Adopting their frames (“Mass Migration” vs. “Refugee Crisis”).
  • Conclusion: Normalisation happens before the vote is even cast, in the pages of the tabloids.

Dimension III: Temporal Dynamics

Theory A: The “Thermostat” (Re-Stigmatisation)

  • Premise: If accommodation fails, MSPs can just switch back to Isolation (Cordon Sanitaire).
  • Logic: “We tried talking to them, it didn’t work, now we will ostracise them again.”
  • Assumption: Stigma is a switch you can turn on and off.

Theory B: The “One-Way Street” (Balcells et al. 2025)

  • The Experiment: Can you re-stigmatise a party (like Vox in Spain) after it has been normalised?
  • The Finding: No.
  • Once the taboo is broken, attempts to re-impose stigma fail or backfire.
  • Why?
    • Voters have already seen the party as a legitimate partner.
    • Calling them “undemocratic” now sounds like a strategic lie, not a moral truth.

The “Stimulus” Effect (Ammassari 2024)

  • Pre-Normalisation: Stigma works. It scares off moderate voters (Social Desirability Bias).
  • Post-Normalisation: Stigma fails.
    • For core supporters, attacks from the mainstream act as a stimulus.
    • “They hate us because we tell the truth.”
  • The Result: The “Cordon Sanitaire” only works if you never break it. Once broken, it cannot be repaired.

Synthesis & Seminar Prep

The Grand Puzzle for Discussion

  • The Rational Trap:
    • If you ignore them → Media might amplify them (Foos & Bischof).
    • If you accommodate them → You legitimise their ideas (Daur) and often lose votes (Krause et al.).
    • If you attack them → You might stimulate their base (Ammassari).
  • Discussion Q1: Is there any successful strategy for a Mainstream Party today?
  • Discussion Q2: Look at your case study. Did the “Cordon Sanitaire” break? If so, did it follow the “One-Way Street” logic?

Key Takeaways

  1. Meguid’s Model is flawed: Empirical evidence shows accommodation rarely works (Krause et al.) and ignores normative legitimacy.
  2. Media matters: It shapes the battlefield before the battle begins (Foos & Bischof).
  3. Irreversibility: Normalisation is a ratchet mechanism. It creates a new baseline for what is “acceptable” in democracy.
