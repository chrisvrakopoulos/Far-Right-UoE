Seminar

In-Class Activities

You will be assigned to analyze one country from Southern Europe: Italy, Spain, Greece, or Portugal.

The lecture identifies five major puzzles in Southern European far-right trajectories (crisis timing variation, historical durability, supply-demand relationships, mainstream strategy effects, etc.). Your task is to investigate these puzzles by developing preliminary explanations for your country, which we’ll test against other regions in Weeks 2-6.

Using the lecture and assigned readings, explore your country’s trajectory to develop three working hypotheses:

On Teams Whiteboard, record your analysis using the following structure:

Country: [Name]

Voter hypothesis: Who voted far-right and why?

Party strategy: What made this party succeed or fail?

Mainstream strategy: How should mainstream parties have responded?

Present your findings to the seminar (3 minutes). Then consider the broader pattern: Do all four countries follow the same logic, or do different factors seem to explain different cases? Record your observations on Whiteboard.

What’s next

Your hypotheses will be tested against other regions (Weeks 2,3, 4 & 6). As we accumulate evidence, we’ll refine and generalize our working theory. In Weeks 7-10, we’ll compare your findings to academic theories to see how we should explain Far Right support.
