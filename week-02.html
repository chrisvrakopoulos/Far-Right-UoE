Seminar

In-Class Activities

You will be assigned to analyze one country from Western Europe: France, Germany, Austria, Netherlands, or Belgium.

The lecture identifies five major puzzles in Western European far-right trajectories (timing differences, government effects, cordon sanitaire paradoxes, etc.). Your task is to investigate these puzzles by developing preliminary explanations for your country, which we’ll test against other regions in Weeks 3-6.

Using the lecture and assigned readings, explore your country’s trajectory to develop three working hypotheses:

On Teams Whiteboard, record your analysis using the following structure:

Country: [Name]

Voter hypothesis: Who voted far-right and why?

Party strategy: What made this party succeed or fail?

Mainstream strategy: How should mainstream parties have responded?

Present your findings to the seminar (3 minutes). Then consider the broader pattern: Do all five countries follow the same logic, or do different factors seem to explain different cases? Record your observations on Whiteboard.

What’s next

Your hypotheses will be tested against other regions (Weeks 3-6). As we accumulate evidence, we’ll refine and generalize our working theory. In Weeks 7-10, we’ll compare your findings to academic theories to see how we should explain Far Right support.

Western Europe - Seminar Notes:

Country Key Discussion Points
Netherlands • Voters: Lower-educated, anti-immigration, dissatisfied with economic change
• Strategy: Jumped on high media attention; centralized leadership to avoid internal conflict
Belgium • Flanders: Success rooted in national cause; cordon sanitaire failure
• Wallonia: FR seen as violent/amateur; redirected to centrist parties
Austria (FPÖ) • Strategy: Shifted from neoliberal to welfare chauvinism; “Makers vs Takers” framework
• Governance: Became less popular once in government; more attractive as opposition
France (FN) • Demographics: Attracting young voters and equal number of men/women
• Reframing: Marine Le Pen reframed republicanism to be more inclusive/modern
